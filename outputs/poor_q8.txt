A) Personality is basically a person’s physical appearance, like their looks and body type.

B) The Big Five personality factors are:
- Openness (to Experience)  
- Conscientiousness  
- Extraversion  
- Agreeableness  
- Neuroticism  

C) 
1. One piece of evidence is that people who grow up in different cultures develop different personalities because of the norms they learn.  
2. Another is that parents’ style of raising children, such as being strict or relaxed, shapes the child’s personality over time.  
3. A third is that major life events, like trauma or winning an award, can cause long‑term changes in someone’s personality.

D)  
- Id: The id is the primitive part of the mind present at birth that is driven by basic instincts, needs, and desires, seeking immediate gratification of impulses.  
- Ego: The ego is just a person’s sense of pride and the image they try to project to others, like how confident they appear in public.  
- Superego: The superego is simply the part of the mind that handles logical thinking and problem solving, like doing math or planning a schedule.
