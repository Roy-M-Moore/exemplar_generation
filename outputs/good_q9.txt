A) Proactive aggression is deliberate, planned aggression used as a tool to obtain some goal or reward, like hurting someone to gain status or resources.  
Reactive aggression is impulsive, emotion-driven aggression that occurs in response to a provocation or perceived threat, often accompanied by anger or frustration.

B) Altruism is when a person helps someone else for their benefit rather than for a reward, like doing something kind for another person.  
In-group favouritism is when people are harsher toward members of their own group than toward people from outside groups.
