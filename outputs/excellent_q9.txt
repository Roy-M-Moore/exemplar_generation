A)  
Proactive aggression is deliberate, planned aggressive behaviour that is used as a tool to achieve some goal or outcome, such as gaining power, resources, or status.  
Reactive aggression is an impulsive, emotionally driven aggressive response that occurs when a person feels provoked, frustrated, or threatened.

B)  
Altruism is when a person helps or cares for someone else and is concerned about that other person’s well-being.  
In-group favouritism is the tendency to give more positive treatment, trust, and rewards to people we see as belonging to our own group than to people who are in an out-group.

C)  
The cognitive error is the fundamental attribution error (also called the correspondence bias).  
The fundamental attribution error is the tendency to explain other people’s behaviour mainly in terms of their internal traits or dispositions (for example, “irresponsible”) while downplaying or ignoring the situational factors that may have caused the behaviour.
