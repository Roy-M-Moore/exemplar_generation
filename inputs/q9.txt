# A) Proactive and reactive aggression (30 points total):
## Defines proactive aggression as planned, goal-directed behaviour used as a means-to-an-end. (15 points)
## Defines reactive aggression as impulsive, emotion-driven behaviour that occurs in response to provocation or perceived threat. (15 points)
# B) Altruism and in-group favouritism (40 points total):
## Defines altruism as intentional behaviour aimed at helping another person, often involving a potential cost to oneself. (20 points)
## Explains in-group favouritism as the preferential treatment members of one’s own group. (20 points)
# C) Attribution errors (30 points total):
## Identifies the error as fundamental attribution error or correspondence bias. (10 points)
## Defines the fundamental attribution error as the tendency to attribute others’ behaviour to internal dispositions rather than situational factors. (20 points)
